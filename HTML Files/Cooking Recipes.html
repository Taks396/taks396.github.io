Cooking Recipes

By Taks

Description

This won't be a long description I promise. This page exists to alphabettically list all of the recipes I have made ever since I started learning how to cook midway through college. I am not a chef, but following these recipes at my altitude seems to work well.

Also, I hate the spiel that most recipe sites put at the beginning of EVERY SINGLE recipe, so the links in the sidebar take you to the ingredients and instructions directly. You can read the background if you want, but thats not the goal.

Finally, here's a quick list of some cooking terminology that I use but always found confusing, as well as some helpful tips:

  • Cutting:
    • Whole: Use the entire item of food. Might be useful for garlic, but I wouldn't know.
    • Crushed: Self explanitory. No cutting but crush the food item with whatever tool you have.
    • Sliced: Slices usually long and thin compared to the entire item. Garlic is like 2mm thin, onions closer to 5mm.
    • Fine/Minced: First slice, then cut the slices into tiny bits. Similar bit size as the slice width.
    • Paste/Pressed: This is kinda like crushing and mincing at the same time. You're trying to get the juice AND make fine bits.
  • To Taste: I give you numbers for seasoning that I find tasty, but it might be strong or weak for anyone else. Get yourself a small fork or spoon and occasionally taste the food. If you're cooking for more than yourself, just rinse the taster or get another one.
  • Temperature:
    • Speaking Range: low...medium...high
    • Numeric Range: 1-2...5-6...9-10
    Depending on your stove, these ranges could be accurate, or COMPLETELY WRONG, so take this advice with a grain of salt.

Alfredo Pasta:


Background

At home, my family never cooked alfredo pasta. Always spaghetti, which don't get me wrong tastes great, but never alfredo pasta. That was always reserved for going out to eat at resturaunts. In college, I wanted to change that, but since I was a poor college student, I couldn't exactly go out to eat often. Hence, homemade alfredo pasta!

Alfredo Pasta: Ingredients

  • 4oz Pasta Noodles
  • 1 Tbsp Butter
  • 2 cloves Minced Garlic or 2-3 Tbsps Garlic Powder
  • 1 Tbsp Onion Powder
  • 1/2 cups Milk
  • 1/2 cups Heavy Whipping Cream
    •
  • 1 cup Parmesan Cheese
  • 1/4 tsp Salt or to taste
  • 1/4 tsp Pepper or to taste
    •
  • 2 Tbsps Finely Chopped Parsley

This should serve 1-2 people if I did the math right. My original recipe is the same but the numbers are slightly bigger to account for some leftovers and the existance of Bijou.

Instructions

  1. Boil noodles in a pot of water on high heat. Some sites will say boil before adding the noodles, but I find it doesn't matter as long as your checking how soft they are once it does start boiling. Drain the water once the noodles are soft to your liking.
  2. At the same time, heat your butter in a sauce pan over medium heat until it starts bubbling.
  3. Add the minced garlic to the bubbly butter and mix around until golden brown or it smells garlicy. Should be about 30 seconds.
  4. Add milk and cream into the saucepan. If you didnt add garlic earlier, add your garlic powder now. Mix until simmering.
  5. Reduce to low heat. Add the onion powder, salt, pepper, and parmesan cheese. Mix until the cheese melts and the sauce is a bit thicker than before.
  6. Remove from heat and dump drained noodles into the sauce and mix. If your saucepan isnt big enough, pour the sauce and the noodles into a different bigger bowl.
  7. Add parsely as garnish if you have it and serve.

Alfredo Pizza:


Background

So this one is funny. I made a deal with Bijou sometime near the end of college that if I could make a pizza, only then could I call myself a chef. Well that came back to bite me and I ended up making a really good alfredo pizza. Why alfredo? Dominos Pizza sells the best pizza in my opinion and my favorite from them is a three meat pizza with alfredo sauce instead of standard tomatoe sauce.

Alfredo Pizza: Ingredients

  • 1 ball Pizza Dough
  • 3/4 cups Alfredo Sauce
  • 8oz Low Moisture Mozzarella Cheese
  • 1.5 Tbsps Sliced Chives
  • Sliced Pepperoni
  • Olive Oil and Flour
  • Salt to taste
    •

This will produce a pizza about the same size as a Dominos medium pizza and can be cut into 4-6 slices, but only feeds 2-3 people.

Instructions

  1. Prepare your dough! If you're making your own, you're on your own here. If you're using store bought dough, follow the instructions on the back to let the dough rise. Mine says to keep it out of the fridge for 4 hours, then let it sit in a light sprinkling of olive oil for 8 hours.
  2. Preheat oven to 500F (260C). Roll your dough around in some flour then spread it over a pizza baking tray or a normal baking tray. Keep enough dough near the edges to make a really flat bowl.
  3. Add your homemade or store bought alfredo sauce and evently spread over your dough bowl.
  4. Pour on your mozzarella cheese and spread evenly. Lightly salt the pizza.
  5. Add a layer of pepperoni slices.
  6. Move the pizza tray into the oven until the cheese is bubbling (10-15 minutes). Some ovens heat unevenly, so I rotate the pizza after about 8 minutes to try to keep the crust evenly cooked.
  7. Remove from oven and add your chives as garnish.
  8. This ones obvious, but cut it up into slices to serve each person 1-2 each. Enjoy!

Ketogenic Falafel


Background

There was a specific part of my colleges' cafeteria that served weird dishes meant to immitate food across the globe. One day, they served falafel with tzatziki sauce, and I wanted to make my own. The day I prepared to cook them, I learned that they take chickpeas and that said chickpeas need to soak in water overnight. I didn't have the time for that, so I found this recipe that uses almonds instead, and later learned it was ketogenic as a result. This recipe also makes a tahini sauce instead of tzatziki sauce so I will list those ingredients and instructions seperately.

Ketogenic Falafel: Ingredients

  • 1 cup Raw Almonds
  • 1/4 cup Hemp Hearts
  • 2 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 tsp Chipotle Chili Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Onion Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Sea Salt
  • 1 Egg
  • 2 Tbsps Refined Coconut Oil
  • 1/2 cup Finely Chopped Parsley
  • Avocado Oil

Tahini Sauce: Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup Tahini
  • 1 Lemon juiced
  • 1 Tbsp Agave Nectar
  • 1 Tbsp Avocado Oil

This will produce 12-16 ping-pong sized falafel and should feed 2 people without a side, 4 people with a seperately prepared side.

Main Instructions

  1. Place almonds, hemp hearts, garlic, cumin, chili powder, onion powder, and sea salt into a food processor or blender. Food processors are better for mixing solid foods, but I don't own one, and you don't NEED one.
  2. Process/Blend until ingredients are more or less a mixed powder. Should take 10-30 seconds. If you're using a blender like me, make sure to scrape the material off the sides and blend again to make sure the mix is even.
  3. Add the egg, coconut oil and parsely and mix. Scrape the sides of the blender during this step as stuff absolutely will stick.
  4. Chill the mixture in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350F (177C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  6. Scoop 12-16 blobs of batter onto the baking sheet and roll them into a ball. Each should be about the size of a ping pong ball.
  7. Bake them in the oven for 20 minutes and prepare your preferred sauce (see below for tahini sauce)
  8. Spray some avocado oil onto the falafel and broil them on the top oven tray for 1-2 minutes until they look golden brown. Results may vary per oven so just watch them.
  9. Serve alone or in wraps and enjoy!

Tahini Sauce

  1. Juice and de-seed your lemon into a medium bowl.
  2. Add tahini paste, agave nectar and avocado oil and whisk until well mixed.
  3. Optional: Add 2-4 Tbsps of water to thin it out, but I never do this because I like the dressing thick as is.

Stovetop Burgers:


Background

Again, my family rarely cooked burgers, and when we did they were awesome, so it annoyed me that we'd do it so infrequently. When I moved out for college, I wanted to change that, but also didn't know how to use a grill. Therefore, stovetop cooked burgers! They end up tasting incredible, but I have never managed to not set off my smoke alarm. Be warned!

Stovetop Burgers: Ingredients

  • 1Tbsb Olive Oil
  • Burger Buns
  • Beef Burger Patties
  • Velvetta Cheese Slices
  • Halved Onion Slices
  • 3-4 Pickles Chips per Burger
  • Condiments
  • Optional Fries

I intentionally left very few quantities in the ingredients list because how many you make will change these quantities. In general, buying any package of those ingredients can create 4-8 burgers.

Instructions

  1. Prep your bun and toppings. Cut up your onion slices, apply condiments to the top and bottom buns, and have the cheese slices ready to put on the burgers while cooking. The burgers will come right off the pan and onto a bun, so you will want the toppings to be ready to assemble.
  2. Add olive oil to a medium sized pan set to medium high heat. Bring it up till it just starts smoking.
  3. Add your burgers and flip them once about every minute or two to cook both sides evenly.
  4. Once they look dark but not burnt, add a slice of cheese on top of each burger and melt it.
  5. Transfer a burger to a prepped bun and enjoy!
  6. Optional: Prepare some store bought fries in an air frier if you have one. If not, theres always fast food fries!

Tuna Melts


Background

The cafeteria at college served these tuna melts occasionally, but not often enough to me or Bijous liking. So one day, I wanted to challenge myself and make something similar at home. In the process, I found out that this recipe makes a tasty tuna salad if you don't want melts. I can't really say it's super healthy due to the mayonase, but it does contain a lot of balanced nutritional items.

Tuna Melt: Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup Mayonnaise
  • 1/2 Lemon Juiced
  • 1/2 Tbsp Tahin
  • 12oz Oil Based Tuna
  • 1 Celery Rib Finely Chopped
  • 2 Dill Pickles Finely Chopped
  • 1/4 cup White or Red Onion
  • 2 Tbsps Finely Chopped Parsley
  • Salt & Pepper to taste
  • 4 Tbsps Salted or Unsalted Butter
  • 8 Slices of Bread
  • 1 Tomatoe Sliced
  • 8 Slices of Cheese

Depending on your size of bread slices, this will make 4-6 tuna melt sandwiches. If you use smaller slices, you can also have some leftover tuna salad!

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk mayo, lemon juice (de-seeded) and tahin.
  2. Drain the tuna and pull it apart using a fork before adding it to the bowl. You want the tuna to look kinda like pulled pork or finer.
  3. Add celery, pickles, onion, and parsley to the bowl and evenly mix.
  4. Season this mix with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Spread 1/2 Tbsp on each slice of bread. These will be the OUTSIDE of the tuna melt.
  6. Add a slice of cheese to the INSIDE of a slice of bread, a layer of tuna salad about half an inch thick, a tomatoe slice, another slice of cheese, and top the sandwich. Repeat for all remaining sandwiches.
  7. Line a baking with aluminum foil and place all sandwhiches in the oven at 400F for 7-10 minutes or the bread is golden brown. You can try to flip them midway through, but I've never done this.
  8. Serve with chips or the remaining tuna salad and enjoy!
