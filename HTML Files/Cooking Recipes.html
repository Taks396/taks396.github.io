Cooking Recipes

By Taks

Description

This won't be a long description I promise. This page exists to alphabetically list all of the recipes I have made ever since I started learning how to cook midway through college. Each one is a dropdown so that you don't have to scroll too far to find the information you're looking for and don't have to look at information you don't need. I am not a chef, as much as Bijou thinks I am, but I have found these recipes to be consistently tasty when I make them. Also, I hate the spiel that most recipe sites put at the beginning of EVERY SINGLE recipe, so the links in the sidebar take you to the ingredients and instructions directly. You can read the background of the recipes if you want, but since that's not the focus of this page, I've hidden them until you click on them.

Finally, here's a quick list, in no particular order, of some cooking terminology that I use but found confusing while I was learning, as well as some helpful tips:

Cutting:

  • Whole: Use the entire item of food, except the inedible parts like onion skin.
  • Crushed: Self explanatory. No cutting but crush the food item with a mallet or the side of a knife.
  • Sliced: Slices usually long and thin compared to the entire item. Garlic is like 2mm thin, onions closer to 7mm.
  • Fine/Minced: First slice, then cut the slices into tiny bits. Similar bit size as the slice width.
  • Paste/Pressed: This is kinda like crushing and mincing at the same time. You're trying to get the juice AND make fine bits.

To Taste: I give you values for most of the seasoning I use, but sometimes I don't because the value would be really small, or I personally find the value doesn't impact the consistency of the taste. I suggest you get yourself a small fork or spoon and occasionally taste the food. If you're cooking for more than yourself, just rinse the utensil.

Temperature: Depending on your stove, these ranges could be accurate, or COMPLETELY WRONG, so take this advice with a grain of salt:

  • Word Range: LOW ... MEDIUM ... HIGH
  • Numeric Range: 1-2 ... 5-6 ... 9-10

Expiration Dates: Check these when you're making any meals. Personally, I don't do this because I have a gut of steel, but after having nearly killed Bijou by cooking with expired products, I have been more mindful of those dates when I cook for other people. Heed my warning.

Cooking Environment: I cannot tell you how to cook according to where you live. I live at a high elevation and humidity varies wildly here which changes my results compared to someone at sea level. I also don't travel, so I have no idea how to cook when the environment is vastly different. For standard cooking, this shouldn't matter as long as you just check the food and adjust your temperature and time accordingly, but with baking, the best advice I can give is to find a religion and start praying to a god that will smite both you and your first born.

Alfredo Pasta

Background

At home, my family never cooked alfredo pasta. Always spaghetti. Don't get me wrong, I like that spaghetti, but I LOVE the taste of alfredo, and only ever got it at restaurants. In college, I wanted to change that, but since I was a poor college student, I couldn't exactly go out to eat often. Hence, homemade alfredo pasta!

Alfredo Pasta: Ingredients

  • 4oz Pasta Noodles
  • 1 Tbsp Butter
  • 2 Cloves Minced Garlic or 2-3 Tbsps Garlic Powder
  • 1 Tbsp Onion Powder
  • 1/2 cups Milk
  • 1/2 cups Heavy Whipping Cream
    •
  • 1 Cup Parmesan Cheese
  • Salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste
    •
  • 2 Tbsps Finely Chopped Parsley

This should serve 1-2 people if I did the math right. My original recipe is the same but the numbers are slightly bigger to account for some leftovers and Bijous existence.

Instructions

  1. Boil noodles in a pot of water on high heat. Some sites will say boil before adding the noodles, but I find it doesn't matter as long as your checking how soft they are once in a while. Drain the water once the noodles are soft.
  2. At the same time, heat your butter in a sauce pan over medium heat until it starts bubbling.
    3.
  3. Add the minced garlic to the bubbly butter and mix around until golden brown or it smells like garlic. Should be about 30 seconds.
  4. Add milk and cream into the saucepan. If you didn't add garlic earlier, add your garlic powder now. Mix until simmering.
  5. Reduce to low heat. Add the onion powder, salt, pepper, and parmesan cheese. Mix until the cheese melts and the sauce is a bit thicker than before.
  6. Remove from heat and dump drained noodles into the sauce and mix. If your saucepan isn't big enough, pour the sauce and the noodles into a different bigger bowl.
  7. Add parsley as garnish if you have it and serve.

Alfredo Pizza

Background

So this one is funny. I made a deal with Bijou sometime near the end of college that if I could make a pizza, only then could I call myself a chef. Well that came back to bite me and I ended up making a really good alfredo pizza. Why alfredo? Dominos Pizza sells the best pizza in my opinion and my favorite from them is a three meat pizza with alfredo sauce instead of standard tomato sauce.

Alfredo Pizza: Ingredients

  • 1 Ball Pizza Dough
  • 3/4 Cups Alfredo Sauce
  • 8oz Low Moisture Mozzarella Cheese
  • 1.5 Tbsps Sliced Chives
  • Sliced Pepperoni
  • Olive Oil and Flour
  • Salt to taste

This will produce a 10-12 inch pizza and only feeds 2-3 people in my experience.

Instructions

  1. Prepare your dough! If you're making your own, you're on your own here. If you're using store bought dough, follow the instructions on the back to let the dough rise. Mine says to keep it out of the fridge for 4 hours, then let it sit in a light sprinkling of olive oil for 8 hours.
  2. Preheat oven to 500F (260C). Roll your dough around in some flour then spread it over a pizza baking tray or a normal baking tray. Keep enough dough near the edges to make a really flat bowl.
  3. Add your homemade or store bought alfredo sauce and evenly spread over your dough bowl.
  4. Pour on your mozzarella cheese and spread evenly. Lightly salt the pizza.
  5. Add a layer of pepperoni slices.
  6. Move the pizza tray into the oven until the cheese is bubbling (10-15 minutes). Some ovens heat unevenly, so I rotate the pizza after about 8 minutes to try to keep the crust evenly cooked.
  7. Remove from oven and add your chives as garnish.
  8. This ones obvious, but cut it up into slices to serve each person 1-2 each. Enjoy!

Chocolate Mousse

Background

The motivation behind this recipe was extremely simple: I saw a few instagram reels saying you could make a chocolate mousse with hard boiled eggs and thought "Thats dumb, lets try it." Now, some of you, mostly Bijou, will look at this recipe and think, "Hol' up, that's a chocolate pudding, not a mousse! You lied to me Taks!" You are in fact mistaken. The recipe I used CLEARLY says that this is a mousse, so therefore it must be a mousse.

Chocolate Mousse: Ingredients

  • 8 Eggs Hard boiled
  • 1 Cup Milk
  • 4 Tbsp Cocoa Powder
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 1/2 Cup Maple Syrup
  • 1/2 Cup Chocolate Chips
  • 1/2 Cup Nutella
  • 1 tsp Salt

This will produce about 40-50oz of chocolate mousse which I served in glasses that look like they are twice the size of a shot glass. It made three of those glasses worth, but I don't actually know the amount, so I'm just estimating here.

Instructions

  1. Place your eggs in enough water to cover them and bring to a boil.
  2. Once boiling, remove them from the heat and cover for 9-12 minutes.
  3. Place the eggs in an ice bath for 5-10 minutes, then peel them.
  4. Place the peeled eggs and remaining ingredients in a blender and mix until combined, scraping the sides occasionally to prevent clumps from not mixing.
  5. Add other ingredients like more cocoa powder, other types of chocolate, cookie pieces, and more as you see fit. This is really an extremely sweet trash can style of recipe.

Ground Beef Curry

Background

When I made this recipe, I didn't want to learn how to cook chicken and pork almost always tastes dry to me. Bijous mom also told me one day she really likes Indian food, so I wanted to try to make a curry for them. This is why I went with ground beef curry, even though using anything beef in Indian food is wrong. Being not-indian, I don't care and think this recipe tastes great.

Ground Beef Curry: Ingredients

  • 1 lb Ground Beef
  • 1 Onion Chopped
  • 2 Tbsps Olive Oil
  • 1 Clove Minced Garlic
  • 1 Tbsp Ginger Grated
  • 2 Tbsps Tomato Paste
  • 2 Tbsps Curry Powder
  • 1 1/2 Tsp Cayenne or Paprika Powder
  • 1 Tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 Cup Coconut Milk
  • 1 Cup Chicken Broth
  • Salt & Pepper to taste
  • 2 Tbsps Lemon Juice
  • 2 Cups White Rice

This should produce enough for 4-6 people depending on whether Bijous mom wants seconds.

Instructions

  1. Prepare your ingredients! Seriously, you add stuff to the pan very quickly so you don't have time to prep in between. This includes the chicken broth if you have to make it separately.
  2. Add cooking oil to a wide and deep pan on medium high heat.
  3. Once oil starts to smoke or bubble, add onion and saute until they are translucent and soft.
  4. Add ginger and garlic and saute for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add tomato paste and all spices.
  6. Add coconut milk, chicken broth, and lemon juice. Boil for 15 minutes.
  7. While the curry itself is boiling, boil your rice according to the package. I did this in the same pot as I prepared the chicken broth, so my rice got flavor infused.
  8. Layer curry on top of rice and serve.

Ketogenic Falafel

Background

There was a specific part of my colleges' cafeteria that served weird dishes meant to imitate food across the globe. One day, they served falafel with tzatziki sauce, and I wanted to make my own. The day I prepared to cook them, I learned that they take chickpeas which need to soak in water overnight. I didn't have the time for that, so I found this recipe that uses almonds instead, and later learned it was ketogenic as a result. This recipe also makes a tahini sauce instead of tzatziki sauce so I will list those ingredients and instructions separately.

Ketogenic Falafel: Ingredients

  • 1 Cup Raw Almonds
  • 1/4 Cup Hemp Hearts
  • 2 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 Tsp Chipotle Chili Powder
  • 1/2 Tsp Onion Powder
  • 1/2 Tsp Sea Salt
  • 1 Egg
  • 2 Tbsps Refined Coconut Oil
  • 1/2 Cup Finely Chopped Parsley
  • Avocado Oil

Tahini Sauce: Ingredients

  • 1/3 Cup Tahini
  • 1 Lemon juiced
  • 1 Tbsp Agave Nectar
  • 1 Tbsp Avocado Oil

This will produce 12-16 ping-pong sized falafel and should feed 2 people without a side, 4 people with a separately prepared side.

Main Instructions

  1. Place almonds, hemp hearts, garlic, cumin, chili powder, onion powder, and sea salt into a food processor or blender. Food processors are better for mixing solid foods, but I don't own one, and you don't NEED one.
  2. Process/Blend until ingredients are more or less a mixed powder. Should take 10-30 seconds. If you're using a blender like me, make sure to scrape the material off the sides and blend again to make sure the mix is even.
  3. Add the egg, coconut oil and parsley and mix. Scrape the sides of the blender during this step as stuff absolutely will stick.
  4. Chill the mixture in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350F (177C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  6. Scoop 12-16 blobs of batter onto the baking sheet and roll them into a ball. Each should be about the size of a ping pong ball.
  7. Bake them in the oven for 20 minutes and prepare your preferred sauce (see below for tahini sauce)
  8. Spray some avocado oil onto the falafel and broil them on the top oven tray for 1-2 minutes until they look golden brown. Results may vary per oven so just watch them.
  9. Serve alone or in wraps and enjoy!

Tahini Sauce

  1. Juice and de-seed your lemon into a medium bowl.
  2. Add tahini paste, agave nectar and avocado oil and whisk until well mixed.
  3. Optional: Add 2-4 Tbsps of water to thin it out, but I never do this because I like the dressing thick as is.

Peanut Butter Cookies

Background

For as long as I can remember, I have eaten a really unhealthy breakfast consisting of a spoonful of peanut butter and chocolate teddy grahams. I stopped doing that when I moved out to college, but when I would come back during summers, I would still see some pretty old peanut butter in the house. I would eat PB&Js occasionally, but to my knowledge, my family doesn't eat peanut butter due to my fathers ketogenic diet. I wanted to figure out how to use it, so I decided to make cookies out of it for Bijou and her family. Believe it or not, peanut butter doesn't go bad fast at all.

Peanut Butter Cookies: Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups Flour
  • 1 Cup Creamy Peanut Butter
  • 1/2 Cup Unsalted Butter
  • 1/2 Cup Brown Sugar
  • 1/2 Cup Sugar
  • 1 Tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 1 Egg
  • 3/4 Tsp Baking Powder

This should produce 15-18 cookies depending on how large you roll them before baking. The suggested size is 1 inch balls, but my baking tray wasn't big enough to store that many at once.

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350F (177C)
  2. Sift flour and baking powder together and whisk.
  3. Cream butter and sugars.
  4. Add peanut butter and mix until incorporated.
  5. Mix in egg and vanilla extract then add flour mixture and beat until incorporated.
  6. Roll dough into 1 inch balls and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Flatten cookies with a fork or your hand.
  8. Bake for 10-15 minutes.
  9. Pull them out and let them sit for another 10-15 minutes. They will be soft so this gives them time to finish baking and harden.
  10. Optional: Drizzle some caramel sauce on them because it tastes awesome.

Stovetop Burgers

Background

Again, my family rarely cooked burgers, and when we did they were awesome, so it annoyed me that we'd do it so infrequently. When I moved out for college, I wanted to change that, but also didn't know how to use a grill. Therefore, stovetop cooked burgers! They end up tasting incredible, but I have never managed to prevent setting off my smoke alarm. Be warned!

Stovetop Burgers: Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp Olive Oil
  • Burger Buns
  • Beef Burger Patties
  • Velveeta Cheese Slices
  • Halved Onion Slices
  • 3-4 Pickle Chips per Burger
  • Condiments
  • Optional Fries

I intentionally left very few quantities in the ingredients list because how many you make will change these quantities. In general, buying any package of those ingredients can create 4-8 burgers.

Instructions

  1. Prep your bun and toppings. Cut up your onion slices, apply condiments to the top and bottom buns, and have the cheese slices ready to put on the burgers while cooking. The burgers will come right off the pan and onto a bun, so you will want the toppings to be ready to assemble.
  2. Add olive oil to a medium sized pan set to medium high heat. Bring it up till it just starts smoking.
  3. Add your burgers and flip them once about every minute or two to cook both sides evenly.
  4. Once they look dark but not burnt, add a slice of cheese on top of each burger and melt it.
  5. Transfer a burger to a prepped bun and enjoy!
  6. Optional: Prepare some store bought fries in an air frier if you have one. If not, theres always fast food fries!

Tuna Melts

Background

The cafeteria at college served tuna melts occasionally, but not often enough to me or Bijous liking. So one day, I wanted to challenge myself and make something similar at home. In the process, I found out that this recipe makes a tasty tuna salad if you don't want melts. I can't really say it's super healthy due to the mayonnaise, but it does contain a lot of balanced nutritional items.

Tuna Melt: Ingredients

  • 1/2 Cup Mayonnaise
  • 1/2 Lemon Juiced
  • 1/2 Tbsp Tajin
  • 12oz Oil Based Tuna
  • 1 Celery Rib Finely Chopped
  • 2 Dill Pickles Finely Chopped
  • 1/4 Cup Onion
  • 2 Tbsps Finely Chopped Parsley
  • Salt & Pepper to taste
  • 4 Tbsps Salted or Unsalted Butter
  • 8 Slices of Bread
  • 1 Tomato Sliced
  • 8 Slices of Cheese

Depending on your size of bread slices, this will make 4-6 tuna melt sandwiches. If you use smaller slices, you can also have some leftover tuna salad!

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk mayo, lemon juice (de-seeded) and tajin.
  2. Drain the tuna and pull it apart using a fork before adding it to the bowl. You want the tuna to look kinda like pulled pork or finer.
  3. Add celery, pickles, onion, and parsley to the bowl and evenly mix.
  4. Season this mix with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Spread 1/2 Tbsp on each slice of bread. These will be the OUTSIDE of the tuna melt.
  6. Add a slice of cheese to the INSIDE of a slice of bread, a layer of tuna salad about half an inch thick, a tomato slice, another slice of cheese, and top the sandwich. Repeat for all remaining sandwiches.
  7. Line a baking with aluminum foil and place all sandwiches in the oven at 400F for 7-10 minutes or the bread is golden brown. You can try to flip them midway through, but I've never done this.
  8. Serve with chips or the remaining tuna salad and enjoy!
