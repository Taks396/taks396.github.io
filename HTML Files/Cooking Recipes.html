Cooking Recipes

Description

This won't be a long description I promise. This page exists to alphabetically list all of the recipes I have made ever since I started learning how to cook midway through college. Each one is a dropdown so that you don't have to scroll too far to find the information you're looking for and don't have to look at information you don't need. I am not a chef, but following these recipes at my altitude seems to work well. Also, I hate the spiel that most recipe sites put at the beginning of EVERY SINGLE recipe, so the links in the sidebar take you to the ingredients and instructions directly. You can read the background of the recipes if you want, but since that's not the focus of this page, I've hidden them until you click on them.

Finally, here's a quick list of some cooking terminology that I use but found confusing while I was learning, as well as some helpful tips:

Cutting:

  • Whole: Use the entire item of food. Might be useful for garlic, but I wouldn't know.
  • Crushed: Self explanatory. No cutting but crush the food item with whatever tool you have.
  • Sliced: Slices usually long and thin compared to the entire item. Garlic is like 2mm thin, onions closer to 5mm.
  • Fine/Minced: First slice, then cut the slices into tiny bits. Similar bit size as the slice width.
  • Paste/Pressed: This is kinda like crushing and mincing at the same time. You're trying to get the juice AND make fine bits.

To Taste: I give you numbers for seasoning that I find tasty, but it might be strong or weak for anyone else. Get yourself a small fork or spoon and occasionally taste the food. If you're cooking for more than yourself, just rinse the utensil.

Temperature: Depending on your stove, these ranges could be accurate, or COMPLETELY WRONG, so take this advice with a grain of salt:

  • Word Range: low ... medium ... high
  • Numeric Range: 1-2 ... 5-6 ... 9-10

Alfredo Pasta

Background

At home, my family never cooked alfredo pasta. Always spaghetti, which don't get me wrong tastes great, but never alfredo pasta. That was always reserved for going out to eat at resturaunts. In college, I wanted to change that, but since I was a poor college student, I couldn't exactly go out to eat often. Hence, homemade alfredo pasta!

Alfredo Pasta: Ingredients

  • 4oz Pasta Noodles
  • 1 Tbsp Butter
  • 2 cloves Minced Garlic or 2-3 Tbsps Garlic Powder
  • 1 Tbsp Onion Powder
  • 1/2 cups Milk
  • 1/2 cups Heavy Whipping Cream
    •
  • 1 cup Parmesan Cheese
  • 1/4 tsp Salt or to taste
  • 1/4 tsp Pepper or to taste
    •
  • 2 Tbsps Finely Chopped Parsley

This should serve 1-2 people if I did the math right. My original recipe is the same but the numbers are slightly bigger to account for some leftovers and the existence of Bijou.

Instructions

  1. Boil noodles in a pot of water on high heat. Some sites will say boil before adding the noodles, but I find it doesn't matter as long as your checking how soft they are once it does start boiling. Drain the water once the noodles are soft.
  2. At the same time, heat your butter in a sauce pan over medium heat until it starts bubbling.
  3. Add the minced garlic to the bubbly butter and mix around until golden brown or it smells garlicy. Should be about 30 seconds.
  4. Add milk and cream into the saucepan. If you didnt add garlic earlier, add your garlic powder now. Mix until simmering.
  5. Reduce to low heat. Add the onion powder, salt, pepper, and parmesan cheese. Mix until the cheese melts and the sauce is a bit thicker than before.
  6. Remove from heat and dump drained noodles into the sauce and mix. If your saucepan isn't big enough, pour the sauce and the noodles into a different bigger bowl.
  7. Add parsley as garnish if you have it and serve.
